Chinese fitness platform Keep raised $127 new round

published at: 2018-07-11T21:27:00+08:00

the fitness platform's new round of funding

On July 10,Keep,a Chinese fitness app,announced a $127 new round of funding.The funding was lead by Goldman Sachs and joined by Tencent,GGV Capital,Morningside Capital and BAI (Bertelsmann Asia Investment Fund).Keep had previously raised $60m in five rounds of financing,the last of which was tencent's C+ round.

Keep's CEO,Wang Ning,congratulate his team in a letter.He claimed that the new financing not only was the credit China's fitness industry and ventures gave to Keep,the three-year-old startup,but also was recognition for team members' hard work day and night.

Mr Wang said the financing would mainly be used for research and development of AI products,continuous content production and incubation of creative businesses. Keep has started developing AI products since 2017.The sports platform has rich data of users that provides a good foundation for AI research.Keep will continue to focus on the contents of fitness industry and provide various tutorials.It will also connect more outstanding experts, teachers, coaches, KOLs and fitness enthusiasts to make workout more efficient and more scientific.

Keep's basic information

Founded in early 2014,Keep was just an ordinary fitness app mainly providing some basic workout videos.Keep has come a long way since then.Today,the fitness platform has developed into a multi-dimensional sports platform.Keep now provides users with tutorials,activities,social functions,diet guidance and it also sells fitness equipment.According to data released by Keep in March this year,the number of users on Keep's platform reached 120 million.Keep also created a sportswear brand named KeepUp and sold hardware named KeepKit for families.In march 2018, Keep set up a Keepland,its first offline fitness gym in Beijing.
